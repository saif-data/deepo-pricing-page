You Asked, So We Built It: Import CSV Data to Your Pulse Account

by Kerry Crawford

Posted on: October 2nd, 2014

We’re pleased to announce that we’ve added Pulse’s most-requested feature update – data imports.

We understand that it’s difficult and time consuming to keep your data current in more than one place. You can now save time by importing a CSV of your data into your Pulse account.

Here’s how it works:

1. To start the import process, log in and go to your Cash Flow page.

2. Export your data from your accounting software to a CSV file.

3. Make sure your CSV matches our format.

At the moment, CSVs have to follow our format in order to be imported.

Before uploading your file, make sure it has the following columns:

  • Date - The date of the payment or deposit in the format of YYYY-MM-DD.
  • Title - The title for your new item.
  • Payment* - Enter a field in this column to create an expense.
  • Deposit* - Enter a field in this column to create an income item.

*Note that you can only have one payment or deposit per row.

We’ll also accept these additional columns:

  • Account - The Pulse account the item is to be created under.
  • Company - Enter a field in this column to associate this item to a project under this company.
  • Description - The description of your new item.
  • Category - The Pulse category for your new item.
  • Memo - The memo of the payment / deposit.

You can tweak your existing CSV to follow our format or download our template.

4. Upload the CSV to your Pulse account.

5. Edit your Income and Expenses

After you upload your file, you’ll have the opportunity to edit each entry (or row) from your CSV before it’s imported.

6. When you’re satisfied with your entries, click “save all import items” to complete your import.

After you save your import, your data will be available in your Pulse account. You’ll be able to edit items, manipulate them and get reports on your cash flow, just like you would if you had manually entered your income and expenses.

Our imports are still in beta, and we’re continuing to work on making it easier to integrate the data in your accounting software with your Pulse account.

Give imports a try and let us know what you think. If you’re having trouble importing your CSV, or if you’d like to request that we work on integrations for a kind specific accounting software, please let us know by emailing info@pulseapp.com.
