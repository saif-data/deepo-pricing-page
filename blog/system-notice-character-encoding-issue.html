Best Laid Plans: Our Upgrade Broke Character Encoding

by JD Graffam

Posted on: August 27th, 2014

It’s fixed now, but you can still read about the incident.

Update, August 28, 2014: We believe this issue is fully resolved. If you find anything that does not look right to you, please let us know.

We pushed a major upgrade to Pulse on Sunday, August 24 around midnight in the U.S. This upgrade is something we’ve been working on since October, and it will serve as the foundation for dozens of upgrades over the coming months, including our most requested feature, imports. We’re also finally able to make some foundational upgrades to the performance of Pulse, so it will be faster for all of our users.

But sometimes things don’t go as planned, and we encountered a problem with the database upgrade for many of our customers. The problem is that the character encoding between our old database and our new database is not compatible, which caused many of our users who use special characters to have a small panic attack when their entries, notes and categories turned into gibberish. It looks something like this:

It is worth noting that this issue only affects old entries. Any new entries created after the upgrade Sunday are fine.

If this happened to you, we’re sorry. We are pushing a system-wide fix that we anticipate to have completed by close of business on Friday, August 29.

If you would like to contact our customer service team, please do so by using our form. We will be happy to explain further or help you in any way we can.
